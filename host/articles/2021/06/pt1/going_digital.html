Volume IX

Humanity's ability to agree on a set of rules, principles, and values is unique within the animal kingdom. We’ve shared common beliefs surrounding gods, morals, values, social hierarchies, corporations, governments, and this list goes on.

It seems like we have a predilection for it.

We’re once again stretching the beliefs and cooperation skills of our species. This time, we’re reaching outside the physical --where our senses are most adept-- and going digital.

Ironically, at a time where groups have never seemed more ideologically divided, we’ve managed to collectively agree and generate billions of dollars in wealth based on digital assets.

Nothing like a modern day gold rush to cultivate unity.

During periods of excess, financial markets rarely struggle finding avenues to allocate capital.
But a few years ago, the rise of digital assets would have seemed like a long shot. It’s so esoteric; it’s almost sci-fi.

The block chain hasn’t just gained momentum; its hit warp speed.
The future is digital.
From Sotheby’s to CNBC, no one can escape the inevitable.

Cryptocurrencies, NFTs, and other block chain backed assets have the potential to reconstitute financial markets AND end in a blaze of speculative glory.

Outside of the pure financial gluttony, digital assets have the potential to contribute real value to society.

Banks

Cyrpto and block chains have the potential to be the most disruptive in the banking sector.

Simplistically speaking, a bank is essentially an institution that tracks of accounts and their associated value on private ledgers.
You can think of the blockchain as an incorruptible ledger that everyone can write in, without going through a centralized institution.
This could pave the way for more peer to peer interactions.

Banks have been the front line defense of the US and global financial system, keeping dirty money in or out. Post 9/11, the United States used their prestige as an international currency to drive illicit activities out of the global economy, by denying bad actors access to United States Dollars (USD). This wasn’t completely effective, as it’s difficult to stop the flow of physical dollars (M1 money) . Crypto can upend those dynamics.

China recently released a digital Yuan and has been promoting it for international use. With the volatility in popular cryptos like Erethum and Bitcoin, the Chinese digital Yuan could gain some traction in countries that struggle to get their hands on USDs.

Much to China’s chagrin, the Yuan doesn’t pose a real threat to the United States as a reserve currency. It would completely shatter China’s economy as the world's manufacturing center.

With the rise of digital currencies, banks' role in the financial system could be crippled, rendered obsolete, or heavily restructured. All this lies at the feet of The Federal Reserve.

The Federal Reserve is planning to release a white paper this summer to spur ideas around a Fed Coin. The Fed Coin will likely be the cryptocurrency to end the speculative bubble.

The Fed is walking a tightrope, likely contributing to their reticence on the matter. Anything too overbearing and one of the many dozen cryptocurrencies could rise in popularity. Anything too loose and we’re only inviting chaos.

In theory, a Fed Coin could transform central banks from a bank for banks to a bank for all. They’ve already tipped their toes into this space with the Main Street Lending program, when the Fed was offering loans to business and consumers during the Covid Lockdowns.

The United States banking system could use a refresh offered by cryptocurrencies. An update to a system that was designed so many decades ago.

As the CARES Act showed, giving money to every American is easier said than done. 25% of Americans are unbanked or under banked and distributing money was filled with fraud, delays, and inefficiency. On the flip side, 97% of Americans have a cell phone and 85% have a smartphone. Distributing digital dollars is an instantaneous breeze.

Many cryptos have the capacity to run parallel to a Fed Coin. But, these won't be money, in the same sense that gold isn't money.
For those who value privacy, Erethum will likely be a fan favorite. It won’t be used in the bulk of transactions, and with the volatility seen in the crypto space, we should all be thankful.

Welcome to decentralized finance, a brave new world.

Digital Authentication

The digital and the physical worlds are intermengialing like never before. At the same time, the digital world is more complex and algorithmic than ever before.

Currently the assets we put our faith in are completely based on trust. But, trust amongst disjoint groups is dwindling. The ownerships of shares is only authenticated by your brokerage, and it's not safe from hackers. The provenance of rare artworks is purely based on credbility. In the future, the assets we put our faith in will be blocks of code that are digitally verified.

Sotheby’s once infamously sold a fake Rothko

As an artist creates a unique art, simultaneously it’s logged on the block chain. As the work changes hands from collector to collector, so does the associated Non Fungible Token (NFT), it’s digital complement. Impossible to duplicate. Impossible to spoof.

This doesn’t have to be limited to high end art. It could be done for sneakers, designer clothes, and handbags.

A beautiful end to scammers.

Signaling

To some extent, the advent and popularity of digital tokens/coins will be as simple as peer pressure. As the product gets more popular, friends and friends of friends will want to get in on the action just to participate in the conversation.

Let me into your friend group. We are the same.

This behavior is noticeable and sustainable across all sectors of the economy. From sports memorabilia and jerseys to Chanel bags. We’re social creatures; we can’t escape our nature.

Although our world is becoming more digital, the world we occupy is still physical. There will have to be some connection to bridge the digital and physical gap. Showing off your alphanumeric Private Key doesn’t have the same appeal as hanging a Picasso.

That physical connection could come in various forms. I could imagine people holding rare NFTs are allowed access to exclusive places, events, and early, or exclusive, access to purchase products.

A high end fashion brand could sell or auction these tokens like Taxi Medallions in New York City.

This value creation being done in the digital space represents a huge paradigm shift, and many are along for the ride.

Ah, the beauty of free markets.
