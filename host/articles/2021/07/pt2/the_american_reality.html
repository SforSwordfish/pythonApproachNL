Volume XI

I recently returned from Europe. My first trip outside of the country, and an eye opening experience.

Many Europeans view America with such optimism... It's admirable.

Though many have never visited The States, America has branded itself incredibly well across the Western Hemisphere.

America exudes a certain charisma; it’s the Hermes of countries.

The American image crafted through the export of movies, pop culture, ultra-billionaires, and rag-to-riches Cinderella stories have ingrained themselves within psyches around the world.

When asked about the seeming fixation with America, I was told about the highlights: the economic opportunities and the American Dream. Sadly, I had to inform them that the American Dream is all but a myth today.

America is no more geared toward social mobility than other countries.

In fact, your prosperity is heavily influenced by the income of your parents across all ranges of the economic spectrum, and it is strongly correlated at the top and bottom quintiles. Surprise. Surprise.

A study conducted by the Pew Research Center shows absolute growth is occurring across all socioeconomic classes. But, the growth is largest at the top. Those in the top quintile have seen their income more than double in relation to their parents, while those in the bottom quintile have seen an increase of about 75%.

Speaking in terms of income to asset prices (i.e. homes), children of the lower quantiles are significantly worse off. The median existing-home price topped $350k in May of this year.

As America was emerging to prominence, in the 19th and 20th centuries, large amounts of government resources were spent on infrastructure, as well as research and design. This not only boosted the middle class,it created the ideal of the American Dream.

Coming off the incredible technological leaps of the 20th century, the 21st century promised flying cars and we ended up with Instagram. It’s time to reinvest.

Americans have flourished during times of transition and growth. Since the advent of the internet, there hasn’t been technological innovation on the scale of the railroad, GPS, the transistor, or the national highway project. The next age of growth is now.

President Biden’s infrastructure bill is a chance for America to catch its second wind, and revitalize the middle class. It’s the chance to live up to our ideal, opportunity for all.

Post WW2 and the rise of Globalism pushed America past the low-value, low-wage economics of the 20th century and to solely focus on the high income knowledge work of the present day. We’ve seen explosive growth in GDP, but the denigration of a significant portion of the populace.

We have exported our production and manufacturing to lower cost countries in order to focus on the real money makers.

Not only is our economy paying the piper for such dispersed supply chains. We’re also divided politically by what America has become. There are very few, if any, economic opportunities to climb the social ladder outside of the knowledge economy. Even within the knowledge economy, it’s a steep climb.

Former President Trump exploited the disconnect between the American Dream and reality, and he’s cast an unbreakable spell on the rural middle class ever since.

A national infrastructure package could be a win across economic classes, lowering unemployment and putting dollars back into the middle class, while revitalizing roads, airports, and most importantly American optimism.

Former President Trump exploited the disconnect between the American Dream and reality, and he’s cast an unbreakable spell on the rural middle class ever since.

Although, the current plan may be enough to get the rocket off the ground, it’s not enough to put it into the exosphere.

Biden trimmed the infrastructure bill from the $2.2tn originally proposed to $1.2tn . Perhaps to make it more palatable across party lines. Perhaps it’s democrats hedging their bet. Or, perhaps this is a sign of Biden losing popularity and consensus as midterms approach. New York Mayor hopeful, Andrew Yang, knows just how fast sentiments can change as the economic state of your populace changes. Now is the time to be flexible. Take the small wins and prove your value.

As the money is distributed, states will need to show the fruits of their labor, but the Democrats will need to show that they are the party of the blue collar worker. The future of the Biden presidency will be riding on this.

Donald Trump has begun campaign rallies for the 2022 midterms. With a solid backing from the GOP, any flipping of congressional seats could prove a hindrance to the second half of the Biden Administration, and a hindrance is all President Trump would need to tout Biden as ineffective, weak, and “sleepy”.

America could be back, or America could be in for another series of turbulent politics. Such is the nature and beauty of democracy.
