Mistrust

America has experienced a riot, an attempted coup dՎtat, and drawn articles of impeachment for the second time all within the first two weeks.

To some, this may seem as a complete and total surprise. But, this has been bubbling under the surface of America's genteel democracy for quite a while. Since the inception of Donald Trump's presidency, and quite frankly, the rebirth of the Republican party, Nancy Pelosi and the democrats have positioned themselves as the Antithesis of Trump. A sci-fi good vs evil, if you will.

Since the fallout of the 2008 Financial-crisis, inequality has soared within America, and within a socio-economic system as complex as America, it's difficult to pinpoint exactly how we ended up here.

Even though how we got here may be something of an enigma, history has told us exactly how it will end. From the disintegration of Soviet Russia to the French Revolution. Wealth will ultimately find a way to redistribute itself. .

Trump has continually given his supporters a place to cast their hate, aggression, and mockery, whether it's 'Mini Mike', 'Sleepy Joe', or 'Crooked Hillary'. (I think we're seeing a trend here.) If Trump was anything to his base, it was a man who was smart enough to see who the enemy was and the balls to tell the 'truth'. Objectively, if Mr. Trump is anything, it's a liar.

The factors that have crushed the middle class and working class of America, both black and white, are complex, nebulous, and range well beyond any singular idea (or politician, for that matter). But to true believers of 'Trumpism', their god has just fallen from heaven. What we saw at the Capitol on January 6 was a culmination of desperation and rage reaching a boiling point. 

Mr. Trump has eroded the trust within the media, the government, and fellow Americans. Most Americans live in news deserts and are limited in their sources. As a result, most have turned to social media for their trusted source of information, which only plays to amplify and reinforce our beliefs.

As Joe Biden looks to unite a country. That hasn't been so visibly divided since the Civil Rights. America is teetering on an inflection point. There's only one place to start, the bare truth.

As the facts are increasingly harder to find, it appears our society has enjoyed this euphoria created by our own ignorance.

America, as a whole, has a hard time looking in the mirror and confronting the extent of our racist roots. American history is covered in school, but slavery is forgotten. Jim Crow is mentioned, but the reverberations that are still felt trembling through our day-to-day lives are ignored, with many arguing America has grown past racism and transformed into a classist society.

The facts point to a different picture.

If there's anything that's the epitome of being American, it's the American dream and subsequently its capitalist foundation.

Since 1619 when the first slaves arrived in America, black Americans have been exiled from the American dream. Continually trading one form of slavery for another. Whether it's sharecropping, Jim Crow, or the ramifications of the crack epidemic, which ultimately fueled the 1994 Crime Bill and privately owned prisons. Only to be institutionalized and the cycle repeated.

In the background, there's been a wave- or tsunami -of affirmative action from all the powers that be: Joe Biden, Jerome Powell, even the Nasdaq, looking to undo what took centuries to build.

The Cabinet

Joe Biden has declared to be the most diverse cabinet of any presidency, likely to cause backlash from the rural conservative who will, once again, feel left and forgotten by the Democrats. That won't bode well for unity.

The cabinet picks Joe Biden has currently chosen are excellent candidates. However, by highlighting that diversity, not credentials, as the main goal calls into question their fitness and waters down credibility.

Americans (black or white) just want a fair shake. The notion that a man can pull himself up by his bootstraps is starting to sound like a fairytale. America is idealized to be a meritocracy, but it's obfuscated by relationships, family-connections, and quid-pro quo.

No one wants to be there just to check a diversity box.

The Federal Reserve

The Fed has a mandate from congress to maximize unemployment and control inflation.

The maximum unemployment is a squishy figure. Undebatable, unemployment for black Americans is consistently and significantly higher than for white Americans.

Staying unemployed is crucial to building wealth, particularly during economic down turns. Economic shocks have a multiplier effect to black unemployment.

Undoubtedly, the Fed's actions significantly reduce the unemployment rate across the board, but as the Fed raises interest rates as the economy reaches 'max employment' many black Americans still remain unemployed.

The Fed controlling the credit extended to lower incomes and minorities can do more benefit than anything else. Historically, it has been more difficult for black entrepreneurs to get access to credit, even exemplified during the CARES ACT and PPP loans.

The NASDAQ

Multiple studies have shown that diversity often results with improved decision making.

For years now, corporations have voiced the desire to create more diverse groups, but Black professionals account for less than 1% of chief executives and only 3% of executive or senior leadership in the top 500 American companies.

Oftentimes black professionals are placed in HR or ethics departments, riding them of meaningful experience in Profit-and-Loss management positions.

Rather than mandate that companies diversify themselves, it's more interesting to look at why companies don't want to diversify themselves. The benefits are there for corporations and shareholders.

Business and navigating the corporate ladder ultimately boils down to meaningful relationships. A 22-year-old college graduate likely has as much trouble relating to his middle-aged manager, as his manager does with him.

Executives will often complain that they cannot find qualified black employees to fill these roles. This often sounds like bullshit, but I truly believe they believe it. The talent they're looking for is right in their organization, the ability to identify it is the issue.

If data suggests there are advantages to diverse groups, the 'invisible hand' should be all the motivation needs.

To create meaningful change, the NASDAQ will need to do more than mandate a diverse board. There's a need for businesses to start looking for talent outside of the usual places. The talent is there. Promote relationships, have patience and garner conversations with the black professionals in an organization and the talent will show itself.

Irrespective of what these three giants are able to accomplish, black Americans have not ended here by accident. From being denied the tools to contribute to society to the systemic patterns of thinking that are passed from generation to generation, to lift a group out of poverty will take more than superficial changes.

The system is not working for everyone and some are losing faith.

At its core, we're witnessing a breakdown of trust. And don't take this notion lightly, for millennia human beings have gone to great lengths to signal trust to other human beings. From the clothes we wear, to the religions we practice. Everything screams, 'this is familiar, you can trust me.'

Everything about capitalism and our ability to prosper is built on trust. From the transactions we do, the officials we elect, or the idea that tomorrow will be better than today. The future is brighter than the past. This economic machine is built on trust.

Anytime an economic downturn occurs, we're witnessing a malfunction of trust on a systematic level. Banks no longer trust that loans will get paid back and liquidity dries up. But that mistrust is just superficial, the government steps in, calms markets, and ensures all debt. Things start to function once again.  But what we're blindly marching towards the mother of all depressions, a mistrust of government, elected officials, and neighbors. The longer it prevails the harder trust will be to reestablish. Economies can still function without trust, just a lot less efficiently.

The solution to lifting black Americans is the same solution to fixing the country, talking and mutual understanding. The great system of American democracy has never been perfect, and like trickle-down economics changes at the top do little for those in the belly of the beast.

'The power is in the system. The system creates the situation that corrupts the individual. The system is the legal, political, economic, cultural background and this is where the power is of the bad barrel makers. So, you want to change a person, you got to change the situation. You want to change the situation, you got to know where the power is in the system.' - Phil Zimbardo


Good Read

The gift of human beings is our social ability to work together and coexist. Our cooperation in sharing information, ideas, resources, have launched us to the top of the food chain.
Throughout history, Homo-Sapiens have lived in primarily homogeneous groups. Hunter gatherer tribes were typically 150 members or smaller. Modern civilizations are really an experiment to see how far our social reach can extend. Many anthropologists believe that once Homo-Erectus and Homo-Sapiens coexisted in different parts of the globe. Then Homo-Sapiens showed up and completely annihilated the Homo-Erectus. From genocides in Rwanda and Myanmar to the Holacaust. Bad things happen when we can't talk and mutually understand and respect each other's ideals.
Globalism and mutual interest have supported cooperation. But, that's starting to look like an unreliable anchor.
Take some time to talk to someone who may force you to think differently. Someone who may force you to grow.
Malcom Gladwell puts this well in his book Talking to Strangers.

No matter what your perspective. There's so much mystery out there. People who have experienced different things. People who think about life in such unique ways. Stay curious. Stay open minded. Life is a beautiful adventure or nothing at all.
