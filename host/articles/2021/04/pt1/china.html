April 1, 2021

Words by Marlin Rushing



Volume V

The US relationship with China is transforming from frenemies to full-blown adversaries. This has been bubbling beneath the surface for a while, Donald Trump kicked it into the spotlight, and Joe Biden is left dealing with the fall out.

China is an old society, which is difficult for Americans to relate. The Chinese invented gunpowder, paper, and the compass. They were using paper money centuries before the idea took root in the west.

For a people whose history starts in 2000 B.C., they have seen the ups and downs that time has to offer. And it’s undeniable that the progressions of the 21st century are built, in part, on the efforts of Ancient China.

Since the late 90s/early 2000s, when China fully burst onto the global economic scene, China has marched to the beat played by the West. (see: History of Hong Kong)

But having to play second fiddle to the U.S. and the West is no longer in cards. And to a society as rich of culture as China’s, it’s no surprise.

The glaring differences between China and U.S. ideology is reaching a fever pitch and can no longer be ignored for the pursuit of economic growth. Here are my thoughts, each one diametrically opposed to America’s beliefs.

Xi Jinping

President Xi has consolidated power with him as the sole autocrat. In 2018, Xi removed the two-term limit on the presidency and set the stage from him to remain in power for life. China’s future will be paved under the administration of Xi Jinping, and Xi undoubtedly has his eyes set on being the World Superpower, restoring China to its former glory, and making him a very very powerful man in the process. President Trump singling out China was a thorn Xi's side, but nothing he can't overcome.

Now the world is noticing.

Any path towards global dominance won’t be easy.

Hong Kong

After 156 years of British rule and a regime ensconced within a regime, Hong Kong was transferred to China on 1 July 1997.

However, in the last months of British rule, Hong Kong put in place safeguards to protect itself from an overbearing China. In 2019, the Chinese government proposed changes to extradition laws of fugitives in Hong Kong.

Perhaps there were some merits to the change, perhaps not. Beijing's involvement left Hong Kong citizens uneasy. The crackdown displayed against protesters and journalists was the hallmark of a dictatorship and the antithesis of Freedom and Democracy.

Mass Surveillance

In 2020 China introduced a Social Credit System to complement its extensive surveillance system.

There are no private conversations within China. WeChat is the primary form of communication and is laughably insecure. Users have reported being visited by the Chinese authorities for “rumors” or using language President Xi would frown upon.

To receive a SIM card for your phone, you must submit to a facial recognition scan. China has aggregated and heavily mined this data. The regime claims they can identify anyone of their billion plus citizens within 8 seconds.

Regardless if their claims are true, (I’m doubtful) I wouldn’t want to be the one to tempt the President Xi. And it really doesn’t matter if it’s true. As long as people believe it’s true, they’ll behave as if it is.

Compared to most societies, Americans are hypersensitive to invasions of privacy. However, what China is doing is, quite frankly, unprecedented.

Censorship

There’s the internet and then there’s the Chinese internet.

This may sound like a joke, it’s not.

There’s no Facebook. There’s no Twitter. No YouTube, Google, Instagram, or Wikipedia.

A pinnacle of American ideology is the first amendment, Freedom of Speech. Whether congratulatory or critical, all is available. In China, over 8000 websites are blocked by “The Great Firewall”.

Uyghurs

China has been indulging in genocide. The Muslim Uighurs living in China have been forced into “reeducation” camps. China originally denied any involvement, but the proof is undeniable.

Trade

China is without a doubt a massive economy.

They’ve thrown their weight around in this regard. American corporations are desperate for new markets and growth, and China looks like an all you can eat buffet.

Except it’s China who ends up with a full belly and everyone else left with crumbs.

China plays to their strengths and has blocked American corporations from competing, or making it very difficult to compete, with domestic businesses.

Other times, China has American enterprises talking about of both sides of their mouth to appease their customers on the East and West sides of the Pacific.

From Nike, to the NBA, to H&M, all are having to bend over backwards to play in China’s sandbox.

Currency Manipulation

From a manufacturing standpoint, no one can compete with China and that’s no coincidence. China is one of the worst currency manipulators. A weaker currency makes your goods more attractive to exports, but it makes it harder for you to import.

China wants the gifts of an open and global economy, but would rather the rest of the market deal with the curses. Markets don’t work like that. Everyone plays by the rules. You have to give to get.

COVID-19

President Xi would like to move past COVID-19 as quickly as possible. But the West has no intentions of letting this just slip by.

From initially denying any outbreak, the opacity during the crisis, and efforts to quash the investigation of the WHO, China doesn’t want any more details of COVID-19 coming to light.

Joe Biden has called for an independent investigation of the origins of the novel CoronaVirus.

America’s efforts to pull the covers back on Xi’s regime and his refusal to cooperate is the microcosm of America/ China relationship. I feel this is reminiscent of the USSR. China is too much of an unknown to be left to their own vices.

I truly believe America would like to work with China. I even think it’s more beneficial if we all play along. But we can’t agree on the rules of the game. And there’s no game without rules. It’s just chaos.

Taiwan

Hong Kong epitomized Western footholds within the East, and Taiwan is the last holdout. Unlike America, China has never considered Taiwan a sovereign nation. Xi Jinping has declared bringing Taiwan back under subjugation as a “top priority”. Whether China has the audacity to go toe-to-toe with the U.S. and the West is still tbd. I’m doubtful. Public opinion definitely isn’t on their side.

Taiwan is the match that could set the entire house ablaze.


Good Read

When China was emerging on the global scene, there was a lot of optimism about their future. Sure, there were some negatives, but Presidents believed the regime would grow past any major blockers. That didn’t happen. Then it was never particularly convenient to address our noisy next door neighbor.

Let us all take heed. Don’t ignore your problems, like cancer, they only metastasize.

Jordan B. Peterson writes brilliantly about confronting your problems (Your dragons) in 12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos.

We're constantly surrounded by chaos. But we can tame that chaos. Through discipline and focus. Hard work. And most importantly never being afraid to confront them.
