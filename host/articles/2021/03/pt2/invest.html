The Python Approach
March 15, 2021

Words by Marlin Rushing



Volume IV

Short term trading and capital markets are the new reality TV. There’s been a flood of capital to crypto currencies and alternative assets like NFT, that is really reminiscent of Tulip Mania.

Although there’s a lot of trading being done, there doesn’t appear to be an equal amount of investing being done, and perhaps that’s not surprising.

No one wakes up and runs a marathon. We’ve all heard that before. But somehow there’s an obsession with getting rich overnight. So, here’s another one. Rome wasn’t built in a day. The flip side is that it could be destroyed in one.

Being young, you typically don’t have a lot of money or power. Those two are closely correlated but more on that later. But with a life expectancy of about 80 years, young people have time, and that is arguably just as valuable.

I’ve recently gotten into house plants and it helps keep me grounded. More than anything, I’d like to build a new Rome in one day. But life and my plants, won’t be sped up nor will they be slowed down. All I can do is create the best environment and wait.

That’s just like any investment. You can do all the research and strategizing, but at some point, there’s a growing phase. And that’s just a wait and see.

Every year I get older and hopefully wiser, here’s where I’m investing.

Relationships

2020 was a really weird time. The effects of COVID-19, social distancing and working from home really lands the mind in a weird place, like some type of drawn out psychological experiment. With vaccines being available to all American adults in the very near future, I can see the light at the end of the tunnel.

There’s no doubt that humans are social creatures. But what isn’t talked about is the value of that sociability.

No matter how talented you are, no man is an island.

Bill Gates once said, “Steve [Jobs] and I will always get more credit than we deserve, because otherwise the story gets too complicated.”

Successful people surround themselves with very good side-kicks. People who were there to complement their strengths, fill in for their weaknesses, and cultivate new ideas.

Hanging out in the right social circles is key. Hearing about ideas and opportunities is invaluable for anyone looking to grow, financially or mentally

From America’s founding fathers to American industrialists like J.P. Morgan and Vanderbilt, those who forged the future were always in close connection. It could be argued their social network carried them to success.

We no longer live in an industrial economy and, for better or worse, intangibles are more valuable now than ever before. Ideas are the new currency and business is all about relationships.

We are no more than the sum total of friends. They can lift us to heaven or drag us to hell.

I’m investing in relationships. New and old. It’s easier to succeed when people want you to succeed.

Markets

The effects of COVID-19 in economies and markets is often being described as an accelerant.

In some aspects, this is completely true. Deglobalization is taking root and wealth/income inequalities are about to push through the stratosphere. Elon Musk started the pandemic being ultra-rich, $24.5bn in March of 2020, to being ultra-hella-rich in 2021, with a net worth of $175bn.

America is betting big on the future, and a lot of people think Elon Musk, Tesla, and SpaceX is the future. That’s to be determined. One thing for certain, is that humans have never been good at predicting the future.

Periods of acceleration are incredibly disruptive for currently well positioned, encumbered businesses. Markets are, undoubtedly, at an inflection point.

But much as we may think the ground is disintegrating underneath our feet, when the smoke clears, we’ll see it was just splintering.

New trends are forming and there’s an opportunity for new investors to ride the wave. Be an investor, not a trader. Diversify yourself, trends are much easier to identify than obvious winners. (see: Facebook and Myspace)

America is a safe-haven for capital and innovation. Markets may rock and they may roll, but at the end of the day I’m bullish on America.

Self

I’m taking a lot of time to reflect. Really analyzing how I got to where I am right now. Self-awareness is key.

I’m also reading everything I can get my hands on. Hopefully, this leads to more perspective and better ideas.

Working on yourself is the most important work. Although, it’s never measured in terms of USD. Being able to attract the right people into your life, whether it’s business partners, life partners, or lifelong friends. The results are invaluable.

In some aspects, this is completely true. Deglobalization is taking root and wealth/income inequalities are about to push through the stratosphere. Elon Musk started the pandemic being ultra-rich, $24.5bn in March of 2020, to being ultra-hella-rich in 2021, with a net worth of $175bn.

I’ve also been thinking about my value system. In terms of aligning values, the military industrial complex is second to none. Taking 18-year-old kids from all different walks of life and across the country, and molding them into one body. All thinking, breathing, and acting in unison. It really is an amazing feat.

Once you have your values in place. You don’t have to think. You just act; knowing those values are always guiding you.

The Marine’s values are: Honor, Courage, Commitment. Short. Sweet. Simple. Always guiding towards your truth.

Self-work can also be the most difficult. It requires a degree of honesty, and critical reflection. Realizing what your weak points are and the areas for improvement is no easy task. I don’t just mean technical skills; soft skills are a major component as well.

Take care of your mind and body. Exercise mentally and physically. No matter how talented you are you’ll suffer a loss. Make sure you don’t remain defeated.

If you have the advantage of time, seize it. Let the compounding effects of time take hold.
