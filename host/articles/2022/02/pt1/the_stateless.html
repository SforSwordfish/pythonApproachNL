The Stateless

Last article, I wrote about how Billionaires ARE paying their taxes. Or, at least the taxes that they owe.
The tax law just conveniently allowsthe rich to have a low(er) tax bill. Ironically, rather than lawmakers self-reflecting and noticing they’ve been out maneuvered, they’ve managed to turn the tables and redirect public ire. Clever. Never admit defeat.

It would be nice if the ultra-wealthy paid more taxes, but I can’t fault them for following the rules.

If anything, Americans aspire to join the ranks of the elite. Since the first colonizers centuries ago, those who were willing to risk a lot gained a lot, and that’s admirable. It’s embedded into the DNA of Americana.

The idolatry America ha with the super wealthy is turning into hatred, perhaps misplaced. But, absolutely within human nature.

Arguably, America’s fascination with the rich and famous and their fabulous lifestyles has been sparked by the media. Those who flaunt their wealth are not considered crass but rather aspirations. The more flamboyant and auspicious, the better. Billionaires launching to suborbital "space" are applauded, which I find just an incredible waste of money.

The ultra-rich have always lived in a different reality. Mark Zuckerberg's obsession with the MetaVerse may be his actualization of la-la land. Not only does wealth buy luxuries and security, it buys mobility. I don’t mean a penthouse in New York and a pied a terre in Hawaii. I mean true mobility.
A world without borders.

This new age of globalization may be the first period where those with means are supremely free to globe trot. Billionaires float above the conventional borders of nation-states, jurisdictions, and custom agents.
Classic example: Benard Arnault began preparations to move nationality and vast amounts of wealth to France after a “super-tax” was announced.

Billionaires are so mobile, they’re now stateless.

The world of economies has turned from the domestic endeavors of John D. Rockefeller and Cornelius Vanderbilt, and occupies the international (intergalactic?) space of Elon Musk, Jeff Bezos, and Robert Iger, who greet and appease domestic leaders as well as foreign.
To hit the stratosphere, in terms of stakeholder value, businesses have to break into international markets.

Companies are continually forced to bend over backwards and jump through hoops to appease the Chinese Communist Party (read: Xi Jinping).

Elon Musk has thrown heaping praise to China, while harshly criticizing the efforts of the Biden Administration. Perhaps, because, in America, he’s allowed those freedoms.
(This is no way excusing Biden of his blunders)

For the time being, America is a convenient place to live for anyone with vast amounts of wealth. The Rule of Law and policies in place to protect private property are unparalleled.
But, it may not always be. Elizabeth Warren and Bernie Sanders (who has shifted the democratic party extreme left) are aiming to burn the manifestation of inequality (billionaires) at the stake.

One high point of inequality was just prior to the French revolution. As peasants starved, the royals suggested they eat cake. Oblivious to the turmoil just under their feet.
Marie Antoinette became public enemy number one, and her apology was a bit too late. As she laid underneath the guillotine, her last words were, “Monsieur, je vous demande pardon, je ne l'ai pas fait exprès.”
Which translates to: “Forgive me Sir. I didn't do it on purpose.”
She said this to not to the angry mob crying out for her death, but to the executioner after accidentally stepping on his foot. This is incredibly poetic or aprocraphyl, likely both.

Dr. Jekyll has turned into Mr. Hyde and America’s fixation has turned to envy. Americans are looking to eat the rich as societies have done time and time again throughout history.

Peter Thiel has seen the changing landscape, and he’s taken egregious advantages of the tax structure.
Thiel also has New Zealand citizenship, as a “just in case”. (He’s reportedly only spent 12 days there)

We assume despite our differences one bonding agent is our American citizenship. Something we can all align with.
I still believe that the top 0.01% identify as Americans; I just think, unlike most Americans, they’ve got options.
